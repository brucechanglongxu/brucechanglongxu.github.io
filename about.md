After growing up in Pokfulam, Hong Kong for 15 years alongside two ceaselessly happy and energetic [siblings], I moved halfway across the world during the fall of 2019 to start college in sunny California. During high school I was lucky enough to represent my high school and Hong Kong in various [national] and [international] olympiads, overturn my existing knowledge of the [integers], scramble together a [one-shot manga] in a few nights with my closest pen-buddies, as well as involve myself in the [local running scene] with the tightest, friendliest and most ambitious group of runners in the [whole city] - shoutout to Buffco! 

As an undergraduate at Stanford University I am currently pursuing double dual majors in [Mathematics] and [Physics], [Electrical Engineering] and [Computer Science] with a minor in Chemistry. I am fortunate enough to be advised by Ravi Vakil, Giorgio Gratta for Mathematics and Physics Respectively and am intending to be advised by John Duchi and Li Yang Tan for Electrical Engineering and Computer Science respectively. My minor in Chemistry will place me in a unique position to be begin a life-time of learning in the medical sciences in tandem with a strong working fluency with the core engineering disciplines and theoretical Mathematics and Physics. My choice of majors, minors and extracurriculars is intended to best prepare myself for the tech, finance and medical industries; it is also an option for me to pursue a graduate degree either in the form of a PhD, MD or an MD-PhD should the opportunity arise. 

I am a proud member of an amazing team of passionate individuals that make the [Stanford Math Tournament] possible, having served as a power-round writer for 2021. I am also a member of Zuchero Lab, Curtis Lab and an alumnus of Pauly Group at Stanford Medicine and Electrical Engineering. This summer I will join Wakatsuki Lab to investigate protein structures in greater detail with Cryo-Electron Microscopy.
