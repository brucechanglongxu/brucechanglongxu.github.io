I'm a Kiwi. After growing up in Pokfulam, Hong Kong for 15 years alongside two ceaselessly happy and energetic [siblings], I moved halfway across the world during the fall of 2019 to start college in sunny California. During high school I was lucky enough to represent my high school and Hong Kong in various [national] and [international] olympiads, overturn my existing knowledge of the [integers], scramble together a [one-shot manga] in a few nights with my closest pen-buddies, as well as involve myself in the [local running scene] with the tightest, friendliest and most ambitious group of runners in the [whole city] - shoutout to Buffco! 

As an undergraduate at Stanford University I am currently pursuing double dual majors in [Mathematics] and [Physics], [Electrical Engineering] and [Computer Science] with a minor in [Chemistry]. I am fortunate to be advised by Ravi Vakil, Giorgio Gratta for Mathematics and Physics Respectively and am intending to be advised by John Duchi and Li Yang Tan for Electrical Engineering and Computer Science respectively. My minor in Chemistry will place me in a unique position to be begin a life-time of learning in the medical sciences in tandem with a strong working fluency with the core engineering disciplines and theoretical Mathematics and Physics. My choice of majors, minors and extracurriculars is intended to best prepare myself for the Tech, Finance and Medical industries; it would be a dream for me to pursue a graduate degree either in the form of a PhD, MD or an MD-PhD if the opportunity presents itself. 

I am a proud member of an amazing team of passionate individuals that make the [Stanford Math Tournament] possible, having served as a power-round writer for 2021 creating a piece focused on cyclotomic polynomials, the mobius function; motivating and demonstrating beautiful applications under the sharp guidance of Kewei David Lin. Additionally, I am an editor with the amazing undergraduate team for [Comparative Advantage], Stanford's only undergraduate Economics Journal and a committee member of [HKSA] and [Synapse]. During my freshman year I was fortunate to be part of the incredible [Frosoco] (Freshman-Sophomore College) community and the [Stanford Undergraduate Research Association] which aims to connect undergraduates with strong research opportunities. I am currently working on several projects with [Zuchero Lab], [Curtis Lab] and an alumnus of [Pauly Group] at Stanford Medicine and Electrical Engineering for the summer REU program. Next summer I will join [Wakatsuki Lab] to investigate protein structures in greater detail with Cryo-Electron Microscopy. 

In my free time I love to try my best to play Kreutzer on the Violin, Rachmaninoff on the Piano and try my hand at cooking traditional Chinese dishes (not the best, but still entirely capable of outputting edible dishes with probability 1 - epsilon) and of course sketch and run :) I am currently in the process of writing three books titled Mathematical Gems, Physical Gems and Gems in Computer Science which aims to bring the undergraduate Mathematics, Physics and Computer Science curriculum to the general public with greater accessibility. Currently looking for a publisher interested in my work - please reach out!
