This talk traces the trajectory of AI computation through the accelerating arcs of Moore’s Law and Huang’s Law—charting how exponential gains in transistor density and GPU performance have reshaped the fabric of machine intelligence. Bruce Changlong Xu, drawing on firsthand engineering experience across Apple’s M-series silicon, NVIDIA’s Blackwell architecture, and now wafer-scale systems at Cerebras, reflects on the hardware renaissance powering today's trillion-parameter models and tomorrow’s clinical breakthroughs. The talk moves from chip to clinic, exploring how innovations in architectural co-design, memory-compute decoupling, and sparsity-aware execution are catalyzing new frontiers in real-time inference for healthcare, from surgical vision to cancer diagnostics. Ultimately, it argues that we are entering not just a golden age of performance—but of purpose—where the most meaningful accelerations will be measured not in FLOPs, but in lives improved through intelligence that serves.
