This talk delves into the molecular mechanisms driving resistance to asciminib—a selective allosteric inhibitor of BCR-ABL1—in chronic myeloid leukemia (CML). By investigating how structural perturbations in the myristoyl pocket and kinase domain influence drug binding and signaling fidelity, we uncover key vulnerabilities in allosteric inhibition strategies. The presentation integrates insights from AI-guided mutational profiling, molecular dynamics simulations, and next-generation kinase modeling to chart emerging resistance pathways and therapeutic escape routes. Beyond asciminib, this work illuminates broader principles of kinase plasticity, oncogenic rewiring, and how intelligent systems can be harnessed to anticipate and preempt resistance. Delivered at the Stanford Translational Medicine and Applied Research Symposium, the talk highlights a new frontier in precision oncology—where molecular reasoning, AI, and real-time feedback from clinic to bench converge to shape the next generation of targeted therapies.
