Clinicians routinely face patient scenarios that lie outside guideline boundaries; randomized controlled trials cannot feasibly cover every phenotype, comorbidity constellation, or rare condition. This perspective proposes a translational framework that turns population-scale real-world data into point-of-care guidance and outlines the technical and cultural factors that will determine success. We synthesize advances in data aggregation and analytics, including cohort filtering, embedding-based similarity search, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Examples from oncology, cardiology, emergency medicine, and rare disease illustrate how such tools can address evidence gaps and deliver nuanced clinical insights when trial data fall short.
