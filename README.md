**Inspirations Include:** Professor Ching-Chuen Chan (GBS, JP, PhD (HKU), FREng, FIEEE) 

_Professor Ching Chuen Chan, GBS, JP, PhD, FREng, FIEEE_, is a globally renowned pioneer in electric vehicle technology and one of Hong Kong’s most distinguished engineer-educators. Often called the “Father of Electric Vehicles in Asia,” he was the founding president of the World Electric Vehicle Association and a long-serving professor at the University of Hong Kong, where he held the prestigious Honda Chair Professorship and led the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering. Professor Chan’s groundbreaking contributions to integrated electric drive systems and sustainable mobility have shaped international EV standards and influenced policy across Asia, Europe, and the U.S. A prolific scholar and mentor, he authored over 400 scientific papers and 11 books, while also serving as a visiting professor at MIT, UC Berkeley, and Cambridge. For his lifelong impact, he was awarded the Gold Bauhinia Star and named a Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering, the Chinese Academy of Engineering, and IEEE. Professor Chan’s legacy exemplifies how deep technical innovation, rooted in local academic excellence, can drive global change.

​Professor Ching Chuen Chan began his tenure at the University of Hong Kong (HKU) in 1981 as a Lecturer. He progressed through various academic ranks, including Senior Lecturer, Reader, and served as the Honda Chair Professor of Engineering from 1994 to 2002. Additionally, he was the Head of the Department of Electrical & Electronic Engineering from 1994 to 2000. After retiring in 2024, he was appointed as an Honorary Professor at HKU. In total, Professor Chan dedicated approximately 43 years to HKU.

_Professor Tony F Chan, JP, BS, MS, PhD_ 

Born in Hong Kong, Chan completed his secondary education at Salesian English School and Queen's College. He then pursued higher education in the United States, earning both his Bachelor of Science and Master of Science degrees in Engineering from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). In 1978, he obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford University. Chan began his academic career as a Research Fellow at Caltech and later taught Computer Science at Yale University. In 1986, he joined the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) as a Professor of Mathematics. At UCLA, he served as Chair of the Department of Mathematics in 1997 and as Dean of Physical Sciences from 2001 to 2006. During his tenure, he co-founded and directed the National Science Foundation (NSF)-funded Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics (IPAM). From 2006 to 2009, Chan served as Assistant Director of the Mathematical and Physical Sciences Directorate at the NSF, overseeing research funding in disciplines such as astronomy, physics, and chemistry. In September 2009, Chan became the third President of The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). During his presidency, which lasted until 2018, he focused on advancing the university's research capabilities, fostering innovation, and enhancing its global reputation. After his tenure at HKUST, Chan assumed the presidency of King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in Saudi Arabia on September 1, 2018. Under his leadership, KAUST expanded its faculty and student body by nearly 50% and launched initiatives in Artificial Intelligence and Smart Health. Chan also emphasized innovation and entrepreneurship, aligning the university's goals with Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 strategic plan.

As a researcher, Chan specializes in computational mathematics, with interests in image processing, computer vision, and computational brain mapping. He has published over 200 refereed papers and is among the most cited mathematicians globally. Chan's contributions have been recognized with several honors. He is a member of the U.S. National Academy of Engineering and a Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM), and the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS). In 2020, he received the SIAM Prize for Distinguished Service to the Profession.
