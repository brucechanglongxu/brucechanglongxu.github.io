About Me

I’m Bruce Changlong Xu, dedicated to bridging human sensory perception with artificial intelligence. My work focuses on leveraging cutting-edge technologies in computer vision and AI to advance fields like high performance silicon design, ophthalmology, radiology, and oncology.

Currently, I am honing my skills at Cerebras Systems, developing the world's fastest AI processor.

My Vision

My North Star is "Semantic Perception": interpreting visual, sensory, and molecular data to enable proactive, tech-augmented clinical care, enhancing early disease detection in oncology, cardiovascular, and metabolic diseases.

Projects

  • SMI-SAMNet: Mentored by Dr. Jay Park and Dr. Vivek Buch at Stanford Neurosurgery and the Surgeon Machine Interface Lab, SMI-SAMNet aims to revolutionize surgical scene understanding by leveraging advanced computer vision techniques, specifically prompt-engineered SAM (Segment Anything Model) and AdaptIS, to achieve highly efficient, zero-shot segmentation of anatomical landmarks and surgical tools. It seeks to overcome the challenges of limited training data, anatomical variability, and real-time applicability, ultimately enabling scalable, accurate, and intuitive Surgeon-Machine Interfaces (SMIs) to enhance intraoperative decision-making and surgical precision.
  • Asciminib Project: Mentored by Dr. Neil Shah at University of California San Francisco, I am invesigating novel pathways of mechanistic resistance for non-competitive BCR-ABL inhibition.
  • AI/mRNA Integration: Mentored by Dr. Han Zhu at Staford University, I am exploring how AI can enhance mRNA therapeutic design for cardiovascular complications.
  • Spatial AI and Clinical Adoption: Mentored by Dr. Alexander Ryu at Mayo Clinic, I am exploring the intersection fo spatial intelligence and clinical delivery.
