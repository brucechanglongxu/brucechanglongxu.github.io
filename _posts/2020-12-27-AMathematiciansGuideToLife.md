title: Navigating the Professional Career Landscape - A Mathematician's Perspective
layout: post
---

The culture of Mathematics is an interesting one, the happiest and most prolific mathematicians all share one trait in common - an unending curiosity to understand how things work. This is the type of curiosity that enables prolonged thinking, which ultimately leads to profound thinking. I think everyone can learn from a Mathematician's approach to life (regardless of profession) - to doggedly yet flexibly pursue questions that are interesting to them; Once that state of pure inquiry is entered, everything else will follow readily. As adults, let us not forget that the ultimate quest of Mathematics and indeed all of Science is the goal of *understanding*. This helps put professional life in perspective - politics, glory, fame and other fleeting artifacts of human nature is background noise that should not distract from the primary goal of any professional career. Possess an adult's capabilities, but a child's soul. 

*The Two Cultures of Mathematics (Timothy Gowers):* https://www.dpmms.cam.ac.uk/~wtg10/2cultures.pdf
