## Hardy-Weinberg Equation

For a population in _genetic equilibrium_, $$p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1$$ (genotypic frequencies), and $$p + q = 1$$ (allelic frequencies). $$p^2$$ is the homozygoud dominant frequency, $$2pq$$ is the heterozygous frequency, and $$q^2$$ is the homozygous recessive frequency. Note that if a **population is evolving, Hardy-Weinberg does not apply**. The principle states that _allele and genotype frequencies_ in a population remain constant from generation to generation **if no evolutionary forces are acting upon it**.
