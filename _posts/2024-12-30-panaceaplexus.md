The intersection of artificial general intelligence, drug discovery, and biotechnology is rapidly transforming how we develop and deliver life-saving therapies. As someone deeply involved in AI infrastructure, computational drug discovery, and biotech strategy, my career has naturally evolved at the crossroads of biopharma innovation and AI-driven healthcare solutions.From my work at Cerebras Systems, NVIDIA, and Apple in high-performance AI infrastructure to my collaborations with Stanford Medicine, Mayo Clinic, and global pharmaceutical leaders (GSK, NovoNordisk, AstraZeneca), I’ve seen firsthand how cutting-edge AI can reshape industries. My research and partnerships have focused on accelerating precision oncology, immunology, metabolic disorders, and cardiovascular therapeutics — a mission that continues to shape my long-term trajectory.

This post outlines the vision behind Panacea Plexus, a blueprint for where I see my career heading: integrating AI, biotech, and executive leadership to drive impact at scale. My goal is to build on experiences across AI infrastructure, pharma R&D, and venture partnerships to lead transformative change in oncology, immunology, metabolic medicine, and cardiovascular disease. Indeed, I have narrowed down on this path after years of deliberation and understanding my strengths, weakness, native interests and natural aversions. Here is my plan. 

My dream job would be **EVP of R&D, Oncology, Immunology, Cardiovascular and Metabolic Disease** at **Sanofi**. In addition to this, I want to have a secondary role as **SVP of AI Strategy and Partnerships** at **NVIDIA**. Due to my educational history/upbringing, I want to have adjunct/honorary professorships at _Li Ka Shing Faculty of Medicine, Metabolic Medicine (State Key Laboratory)_ **[in my father/mother's footsteps]** and _Stanford Medicine (secondary Stanford Electrical Engineering and Computer Science)_ **[my alma mater]**; whilst being a Venture Partner at _VMS Group (Life Sciences and AGI Fund)_ **[my mentors]**, and Chief Scientific/Medical AI advisor to the _HKSAR/New Zealand governments_ **[my hometowns]**. Hence there are four axis in which I will be developing:

1. Artificial General Intelligence (SVP, AI Strategy @ NVIDIA)
2. Global Pharmaceutical R&D (EVP R&D Oncology, Immunology, Cardiometabolic @ Sanofi)
3. Academia (HKU Medicine, Stanford Medicine and Engineering) 
4. Entrepreneurial Venture Investing (3Cap AGI, VMS Group)

_How do I get there?_ Right now I hold a BS/MS from Stanford University, and have tenures at Apple, NVIDIA and Cerebras Systems. My primary goal within the next **seven years** is to reach **distinguished engineer** level at Cerebras (less so management, more so having very strong technical foundations) (which according to historical data will take approximately six and a half years). Hence I should stay in this role for the duration of my 7 year MD/PhD. During this time, I would like to publish **3 high-impact papers a year** with my close collaborators at the _Buch Lab (Stanford Neurosurgery)_, _Han Zhu Lab (Stanford Translational Cardio-oncology)_, _Neil Shah Lab (UCSF Translational Oncology)_, _Alexander Ryu Lab (Mayo Clinic AI and Internal Medicine)_. Hence by 2032, I will have around **21 high impact papers** and an MD/PhD, whilst achieving distinguished engineer status at Cerebras Systems. 

Then I will take up a faculty position at HKU Medicine, an adjunct position at Stanford Medicine/Engineering, whilst continuing my connection with 3Cap AGI Fund/VMS Group. Right after my MD/PhD I will start my ascent at Sanofi to become Executive Vice President of R&D (Oncology, Immunology and Cardiometabolic), and Senior Vice President of AI Strategy at Cerebras Systems.
