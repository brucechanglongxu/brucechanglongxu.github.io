The cell cycle has two major phases: interphase (G0, G1, S, G2) and the mitotic phase (M). It is an ordered sequence of events involving cell growth and cell division that produces two new daughter cells. A series of precisely timed and carefully regulated stages of growth, DNA replication, and division that produces two identical (clone) cells. 

## Cell Cycle Control

The cell cycle is controlled by mechanisms both internal and external to the cell, and by regulator molecules. It is controlled at three external checkpoints; Damage to DNA and other external factors are evaluated at the **G1 checkpoint**. If conditions are inadequate, the cell will not be allowed to continue to the S phase of interphase.
