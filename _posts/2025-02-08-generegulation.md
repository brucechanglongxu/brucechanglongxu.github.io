## Prokaryotic Gene Regulation (Operon Model)

Prokaryotic gene regulation is _efficient and rapid_, ensuring that bacteria only express genes when needed. The best models for understanding prokaryotic gene regulation are **operons**, which consist of:

1. **Promoter:** The site where RNA polymerase binds. 
2. **Operator:** The _"on/off switch"_ where repressors bind to block transcription. 
3. **Structural Genes:** The genes being transcribed and translated. 
4. **Regulatory Genes:** These encode repressors or activators that regulate the operon. 

Operons allow for coordinate expression of multiple genes under a single promoter, making gene expression efficient. 

**Lac Operon (Inducible System)** 

The lac operon controls the expression of lactose metabolism genes (lacZ, lacY, lacA) in E. coli. This operon is _inducible_, meaning that it is normally off, but turns on when lactose is present.
