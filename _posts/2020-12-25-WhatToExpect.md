*What to expect from this blog*: Daily blog posts about some STEM-related topic relevant to my own academic journey and thinking. 

*New Year's Resolutions*: 
* <strong> Sport: </strong> Finish your marathon / triathlon training block
  * Endurance sport is the love of your life, the feeling of pushing your limits and growing as an athlete is one of the most satisfying in the world
* <strong> Nutrition: </strong> Drink a bottle of water every single day, carefully monitor your diet and limit yourself to fresh produce / high energy, nutrient foods
  * We only have one body, take care of it well.
* <strong> Discipline: </strong> Limit your time on the phone to 30 minutes per day
  * This truly prevents you from living.
* <strong> Articulation: </strong> Write on this blog and make progress on manuscripts daily for at least one hour to work towards publication
  * Technical writing is the heart and core of all professors, learn it well.
* <strong> Thought: </strong> Read and Reflect on one cutting-edge paper in each of the fields of Mathematics, EECS, Physics, Medicine and Chemistry / Biology per week
  * If you want to ultimately be a high-performing academic researcher then you should start thinking like one.
* <strong> Technology: </strong> Learn and implement one new algorithm / technology every evening 
  * Whether this is from pre-existing code-bases, well-known algorithms or state-of-the-art models it is good to push your comfort zone.
* <strong> Network: </strong> Meet two new people in various industries or fields every month, and offer something of value to them (Linkedin Connection Requests do not count) 
  * Buy them coffee, take them out to lunch or offer your own perspective on a topic of mutual interest
* <strong> Music: </strong>Practice the violin (or piano) for at least one hour per day
  * You may not be able to discipline yourself to such a consistent schedule, but perhaps amortize 7 hours a week however you choose.
* <strong> Family: </strong> Call home for 30 minutes per week
  * Make sure to be fully present during these calls and forget all your worries or stresses
* <strong> Global Awarness: </strong> Get into the habit of reading new article updates from the New York Times / Wall Street Journal / Economist on a Saturday morning 
  * Think critically about how this knowledge would benefit your hypothetical financial institution and produce some tangible result 
* <strong> Self-Growth: </strong> Read one new (or old) book every week outside your preassigned syllabus
  * Reflect on how you can apply the knowledge and the lessons of the book into your own life, particularly areas that are weak
* <strong> Language and Reflection: </strong> Write one Reflective Essay in Chinese per Week. Send a message to friends in Cantonese as frequently as you have time for
  * You can post the reflective essay on this blog, or keep it to yourself in written format in terms of a journal
* <strong> Language and Reflection: </strong> Write one Reflective Essay in German or Japanese per Week
  * This one you should keep to yourself as the quality of your writing will most likely be embarassing initially (Aller Anfang ist Schwer!)
* <strong> Arts: </strong> Advance your arts portfolio every week on a Sunday Morning or Evening
  * Good for the heart and the soul, it is nice to produce things freely that you will ultimately be proud of 
* <strong> Productivity: </strong> Complete ten 50/10 Pomodoro blocks of academic-related work/focus every day

Every evening I will reflect on whether I have accomplished my goals for the day, aligned with my New Year's Resolutions. At the end of each month I will also reflect on whether how the entire four weeks has gone and continue to think deeply about how I can improve for the next month. Lastly, it's important that I send my prayers to my family, friends and loved ones on a daily basis and give thanks for the sustenance that I receive from benefactors. By the end of the year, I hope that I can say that it was a year that was lived well; You never know - it might be your last! 

Each of the above criterion can be rated on a scale from $1$ to $5$ from worst to best respectively, you can maintain a colour-coded spreadsheet that will keep you accountable throughout the year and show you areas in which you can improve. 

The theme of this year is the following: *To take pride in doing the things you do well*
