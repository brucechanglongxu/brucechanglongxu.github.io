Generative AI has revolutionized how we create and manipulate images, with models like DALL-E and Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs) leading the way. These models enable the synthesis of high-quality images from text prompts, opening up new frontiers in AI-driven creativity.While DALL-E leverages a transformer-based architecture to generate images directly from textual descriptions, Latent Diffusion Models introduce a more efficient approach by operating in a lower-dimensional latent space, significantly reducing computational costs while maintaining image fidelity.In this post, we’ll explore how latent diffusion works, why it is more efficient than pixel-space diffusion, and how DALL-E and similar models leverage these advancements to push the boundaries of AI-generated art.
