Glycolysis and Gluconeogenesis are highly interconnected with fatty acid oxidation, amino acid metabolism, and the citric acid cycle, which makes them critical for metabolic regulation and energy homeostasis. 

## Glycolysis: The First Step in Energy Extraction

This occurs in the cytoplasm, and breaks down one glucose (C6) into two pyruvate (C3) molecules, generating ATP and NADH. It is anaerobic, meaning it can proceed without oxygen.

## Cori Cycle: Recycling Lactate to Glucose

During anaerobic glycolysis (e.g. exercise, hypoxia, RBC metabolism), lactate builds up and is sent to the liver for gluconeogenesis. 1. Muscle produces lactate from pyruvate (via Lactate Dehydrogenase) 2. Liver converts lactate back into glucose through gluconeogenesis 3. Glucose is sent back to the muscle for energy. 

**The Cori cycle allows ATP production in anaerobic tissues while preventing metabolic acidosis**
