What if your brain and a speech AI model shared the same language—not in words, but in how they represent words, sounds, and meaning? A new study published in Nature Human Behaviour makes a bold claim: the internal representations of OpenAI’s Whisper model—originally built to transcribe audio—align remarkably well with how our brains process language in real-life conversations. Not in lab-controlled, one-word-at-a-time settings. But in the wild: hospital rooms, casual chats, real-world chaos. And the implications are huge.
