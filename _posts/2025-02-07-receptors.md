GPCRs are a large family of _membrane-bound_ receptors that mediate cellular responses to hormones, neurotransmitters, and environmental stimuli. They activate **intracellular** signaling cascades through G proteins. Some of the key features of GPCRs are the following: 

1. **7-transmembrane domain structure**
2. **Extracelular ligand-binding domain**
3. **Intracellular domain interacts with a heterotrimeric G protein (Ga, GB, Gy)**
4. **Activates second messenger**

## GPCR Activation Mechanism

1. Ligand binds to the extracellular domain of the GPCR.
2. Conformational change activates the G protein inside the cell. 
3. GDP is replaced by GTP on the Ga subunit, and Ga dissociates from GBy. 
4. Ga activates (or inhibits) an effector enzyme (adenylyl cyclase, phospholipase C).
5. Effector enzyme produces second messengers (cAMP, IP3, DAG, Ca2+)
6. Second messengers amplify the signal and activate downstream targets (e.g. PKA, PKC)
7. Signal terminates -- Ga hydrolyzes GTP to GDP (through intrinsic GTPase activate), and Ga reassociates with GBy and the GPCR resets. 

## GPCR Signaling Pathways

GPCRs are classified by their Ga subunit type. Each activates different second messengers and cellular response. 

1. **Gas (stimulatory):** This increases intracellular cAMP levels which leads to PKA activation. Subsequent metabolic activation is induced (glycogen breakdown, lipolysis, and HR increase). Examples include _B1, B2 adrenergic receptors, Glucagon, TSH, and Dopamine D1_. 
2. **Gai (inhibitory):** This decreases intracellular cAMP levels which inhibits PKA. Subsequently metabolic inhibition is induced (decrease in heart rate, decrease in insulin and decrease in neurotransmitter release). Examples include _a2 adrenergic receptors, Dopamine D2, and Somatostatin_. 
3. **Gaq (activating):** This leads to an increase in IP3, DAG, and Ca2+, which leads to PKC activation. This leads to smooth muscle contraction, and hormone secretion. Examples include _a1 adrenergic receptors, M1/M3 receptors and Angiotensis II_. 

If we see **cAMP** we should think **Gas (stimulates)** or **Gai (inhibits)**. If we see IP3/DAG, wethink **Gaq**.
