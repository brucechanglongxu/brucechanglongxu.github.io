This is the personal website of **Bruce Changlong Xu**. 

Mathematics, Technology and Medicine
------


In the stillness between signal and cell, I search for a shared grammar—where circuits hum with intention, and the body reveals its quiet algorithms. My work lives at the boundary of precision and perception: shaping the tools that see, sense, and guide—across surgical theaters, learning machines, and molecular maps. I believe in a future where intelligence is both engineered and empathetic. Where computation heals, and insight moves seamlessly between silicon and biology. This site is a record of that journey—of experiments in code and care, of systems that learn, and sciences that listen. A home for the questions that whisper across disciplines—and the architectures we build to answer them. Welcome.

About Me
------

My story begins in Auckland, New Zealand—on the far edge of the world—where I was born to two physician-scientists whose devotion to both their family and their fields shaped every chapter that followed. With no command of English but an unshakable resolve, they emigrated westward in search of opportunity, carrying with them a reverence for learning and a belief in the future. My father, a pioneering endocrinologist and the Rosie TT Young Endowed Professor of Endocrinology at the Li Ka Shing Faculty of Medicine, helped lay the foundation for modern endocrine care in Asia. My mother, a relentless force in cardiovascular science and metabolic medicine, has charted her own path of discovery and impact within the same institution. From them, I inherited not only a love for science—but a deep-seated conviction that knowledge, pursued with integrity and purpose, can transform lives.

Growing up amidst the skyline of Hong Kong, I found solace in mathematics—the language beneath all others. It was there I first glimpsed the power of structure and symmetry, earning gold in the British Mathematical Olympiad with a perfect score, and representing Hong Kong at the International Mathematical Olympiad as HKG01. These early experiences gave me not just accolades, but a sense that beauty and truth were never far apart. But my education unfolded not only in numbers and proofs. It took shape on long runs before sunrise, where I learned endurance and rhythm of thought. In music, I trained my intuition—composing in silence, listening for patterns in chaos. In the visual arts, I practiced attention—sharpening the eye to contrast, form, and negative space. These disciplines taught me to perceive structure not only in systems, but in stories and lives.

Fast forward to today, and that philosophy—the fusion of structure and soul—guides everything I build. At Cerebras, I help shape the world’s fastest AI inference engines and wafer-scale systems, partnering with Mayo Clinic, GSK, and Novo Nordisk to advance genomic medicine and clinical reasoning at unprecedented scale. Previously, I engineered silicon for Apple’s M1/M2 teams and contributed to AI workflows at NVIDIA (Blackwell, NeMo, ChipNeMo). I also am humbled to be investing in the future of artificial general intelligence as a venture partner with the amazing team at 3Cap AGI partners and backing deeptech breakthroughs at Groq, Synchron, and beyond. What binds these experiences is a single thread: a belief that intelligence—whether natural or synthetic—can be designed not just for performance, but for purpose. That our most profound technologies will be those that listen, learn, and care.

_Bruce Changlong Xu (& Claude Sonnet)_
